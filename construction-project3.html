Recommendations

In construction, there are many factors to consider when developing a recommendation. They are divided into the following categories:

General
  • When things are spinning, you should not wear gloves
  • Once you're 6ft off the ground, fall protection is required. According to the "100% rule," for each additional foot of elevation, the risk of a fatal fall increases by 1%
  • Competent person - experience and authority to take proper measures on the construction site
  • Sheetrock is a brand of drywall
Worksite Safety
  • Hierarchy of control
  • Elimination - most effective form of dealing with a hazard
  • Substitution - take one hazard and substitute with another hazard that’s less hazardous (ex: replace ladder with scaffold)
  • Engineering control - physical separation between you and the hazard (ex: phone case and phone and ground), lock out/ tag out
  • Administrative control - codes and standards, control access zones (CAZ)
  • Personal protective equipment (PPE) - last line of defense
OSHA
  • General Industry - covers all industries except construction, agriculture, duct work, maritime, OSHA training typically requires 10-hour and 30-hour coursework
  • Agriculture - includes specific training standards, and usually the 10-hour and 30-hour coursework
  • Construction - has its own set of OSHA standards and requirements for workplace safety, that are different than the general industry guidelines
4 General Health Hazards
  • Chemical
  • Physical
  • Biological
  • Ergonomics
Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
  • Hard hats
  • Safety glasses & safety goggles (ANSI-Z 87)
  • Face shields and eye protection
  • Respiratory protection
  • Vests - safety vests, type 1,2,3 (full body suit) (reflective lines vertically - 1,2)
  • Hand protection and foot protection - steel, toe, and composite

After learning about these important factors and getting first hand experience in a construction project, I know that I have to take proper precautions when necessary as well as exert experience and authority when making these decisions.

Project Analysis
Concepts

This course has shown me the importance of being observant and how it enhances awareness and understanding in various aspects of life, both in practice and within my community. A single observation, when turned into actionable results, can have a significant impact in the long run. From sustainable farming to construction skills to technology, actively engaging with our surroundings and recognizing the nuances in our environments enables us to make informed decisions that contribute to the well-being of our communities and the world at large. Ultimately, cultivating this skill enriches our personal experiences and empowers us to drive positive change in society.

Challenges

Even though I have a background in architecture, learning about these tools on-site and in school were very different experiences. On-site, we had to implement our learning into actionable results, whereas in school, we primarily focused on understanding the tools' uses. My challenge was that it was my first time using all of these tools, which required some adjustment and time to get accustomed to them.

Solutions

With this challenge, I made sure to practice active listening and be observant during the demonstrations and in my surroundings to ensure proper precautions were taken. It was also a collaborative effort with my cohort, so it was helpful to seek and take advice when needed.
